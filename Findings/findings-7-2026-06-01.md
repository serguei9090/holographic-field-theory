## 🔍 2. Detailed Performance & Qualitative Analysis

### Key Insights:
1. **Accuracy & Perplexity Gap**: 
   The Transformer baseline achieves higher Accuracy@1 (42.36% vs. 33.16%) and a significantly lower Perplexity (15.35 vs. 30.04) than CHFT v2 Nano. However, it's crucial to note that the Transformer has access to the full softmax attention mechanism over the sequence, whereas CHFT represents the sequence using a complex holographic superposition without explicit pairwise token-to-token attention.
   
2. **Repetition & Diversity Crisis (The CHFT Advantage)**:
   Despite its superior loss and perplexity, the 1-Layer Transformer exhibits a severe **diversity and repetition problem** (Diversity Score of **33.1%** vs. CHFT's **81.9%**). 
   Looking at the generated samples below, the Transformer gets trapped in immediate repetitive loops ("Luna Luna Luna...", "Billy named Billy named Billy...", "shining shining...").
   In contrast, CHFT v2 Nano produces highly diverse text (81.9% unique tokens) and avoids getting stuck in local attractor loops, indicating that the Holographic memory space has healthier associative dynamics.

3. **VRAM & Training Efficiency**:
   CHFT v2 Nano operates with virtually zero footprint on the GPU (**44.5 MB** peak VRAM) compared to the standard Transformer (**2.57 GB** peak VRAM). This is a **57.8x reduction** in memory usage. Additionally, CHFT trained 37% faster, showcasing the lightweight nature of holographic projection vs. causal attention matrices.
