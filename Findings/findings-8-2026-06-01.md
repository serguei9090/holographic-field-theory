## 🔍 1. Root Cause of the Degradation in CHFT v3

Our initial attempt to improve CHFT by incorporating quantum physics principles resulted in a drop in Accuracy@1 from **33.16%** to **5.57%** (near random chance). We identified two critical mathematical and structural root causes:

### A. Positional Frequency Collapse (Learnable $\omega$)
* **The Issue**: Making the positional frequencies `omega1` and `omega2` learnable parameters (`nn.Parameter`) allowed the optimizer to update them. The optimizer collapsed these frequencies towards a single value to minimize short-term local training loss.
* **Why it failed**: In Vector Symbolic Architectures (VSA/HRR), positional binding depends on the **pseudo-orthogonality** of different position phase vectors ($e^{i p \omega}$). When the frequencies collapsed, the positions were no longer orthogonal. Summing the tokens in the context caused massive destructive interference, blending all positions and erasing the sequential order.
* **The Fix**: Reverted positional frequencies to be **deterministic, fixed random uniform buffers** in [model.py](file:///i:/01-Master_Code/Test-Labs/01-CHFT/colab/src/model.py), preserving positional orthogonality.

### B. DC Bias Accumulation (QuantumNorm vs. ComplexLayerNorm)
* **The Issue**: Replacing `ComplexLayerNorm` with `QuantumNorm` (L2 normalization without component-wise centering) allowed magnitudes to accumulate a common positive bias.
* **Why it failed**: In high dimensions, summing positive magnitude dimensions creates a massive common mode DC bias. Without subtracting the mean, all semantic vectors align towards the positive octant, destroying their orthogonality. `ComplexLayerNorm` acts as a **high-pass filter** by performing `nn.LayerNorm(mag)` (subtracting the mean magnitude), keeping the semantic representations centered and highly discriminative.
* **The Fix**: Restored `ComplexLayerNorm` and its associated $\sqrt{D}$ scaling factors in [model.py](file:///i:/01-Master_Code/Test-Labs/01-CHFT/colab/src/model.py), [train.py](file:///i:/01-Master_Code/Test-Labs/01-CHFT/colab/src/train.py), and [evaluate.py](file:///i:/01-Master_Code/Test-Labs/01-CHFT/colab/src/evaluate.py).
