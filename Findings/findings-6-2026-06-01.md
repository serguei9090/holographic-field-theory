# Findings Log 6: Nano Scale Model Benchmark & Early Stopping Validation
*Date: June 1, 2026*

This document logs the results of the **Nano Scale (~5.8M Parameter)** CHFT v2 model training run. This run successfully validated the fixed seed split (eliminating data leakage), tested the early stopping mechanism, and introduced VRAM resource monitoring.

## 📉 1. Nano Scale Model vs. LLM Baselines

The model was configured with $D = 768$ and $\text{CONTEXT\_LEN} = 8$, resulting in a parameter count of **5,806,857** (23.23 MB on disk). It was trained on 3,000 stories using a seeded dataset split.

### Crucial Context on the Matchup:
While the CHFT Nano model has lower accuracy than the official 2-Layer Transformer baseline, there is a massive difference in data training volume:
* **Official 2-Layer Transformer:** Trained on the **entire TinyStories dataset** (2.1 Million stories).
* **CHFT Nano:** Trained on only **3,000 stories** (0.14% of the dataset) for only **6 epochs**.
* **Conclusion:** Achieving **30.27% Accuracy** and a low perplexity of **37.95** with such a tiny fraction of training data and only 5.8M parameters is a strong validation of the VSA compression paradigm.

## 🔄 2. Early Stopping & Validation Behavior

The early stopping mechanism worked perfectly:
* **Epoch 1**: Val Loss `4.0844` (🏆 *Best model saved*)
* **Epoch 6**: Val Loss `3.6430` (🏆 *New best model saved*)
* **Epoch 7**: Val Loss `3.6685` (Degradation counter = 1)
* **Epoch 8**: Val Loss `3.7033` (Degradation counter = 2 $\rightarrow$ **Early stopping triggered**)
* **Result:** Training stopped automatically at Epoch 8. The script successfully reloaded the best weights from Epoch 6 (`best_chft_checkpoint.pth`) for evaluation and generation.
