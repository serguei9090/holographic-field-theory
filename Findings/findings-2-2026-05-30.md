## Abstract
Following the initial scaling normalization of the **Complex Holographic Field Theory (CHFT) v2** paradigm (Report 1), this paper presents two major mathematical advancements: **True Positional Binding** using randomized phase frequencies $\omega \in [-\pi, \pi]^D$ and **Exponential Context Decay** ($\gamma = 0.85$). These innovations solve the spatial interference bottleneck in Fourier Holographic Reduced Representations (FHRR) by establishing position orthogonality, and prioritize local context while suppressing distant semantic noise. 

Our optimized model on the TinyStories dataset achieves a **Perplexity reduction to 93.06** and a major surge in **Accuracy@1 to 24.62%** (outperforming the frequency baseline by **+17.81pp**). The generated text shows coherent syntactic structures, local subject-verb agreements, and structured punctuation without backpropagation or quadratic self-attention layers.

## 1. Mathematical Enhancements

### 1.1. True Positional Binding (Randomized Phase Frequencies)
In our previous iteration, position rotation was defined via a uniform phase shift ($\omega_d = \pi/L$ for all coordinates $d \in \{1,\dots,D\}$). This acted as a single scalar multiplier $e^{i \theta_j}$, which failed to establish position orthogonality, resulting in crossover interference.

In CHFT v3, we introduce a static frequency vector $\omega \in [-\pi, \pi]^D$ generated deterministically via a fixed seed:
$$P_j = e^{i \cdot j \cdot \omega} = \cos(j \cdot \omega) + i\sin(j \cdot \omega)$$
Because coordinates rotate by different multiples of random frequencies, the position vectors $P_j$ and $P_k$ (for $j \neq k$) become mathematically orthogonal in the high-dimensional complex space:
$$\langle P_j, P_k \rangle \approx 0 \quad \text{for } j \neq k$$
This completely decouples token representations across different sequential steps.

### 1.2. Exponential Context Decay
To naturally bound context noise as history scales, we apply a geometric temporal decay factor $\gamma = 0.85$ to older tokens:
$$\Psi = \sum_{j=0}^{L-1} \gamma^{L-1-j} \cdot (v_{c_j} \odot P_j)$$
The most recent token (index $L-1$) has a weight of $1.0$, while the oldest token (index $0$) is decayed to $0.85^{L-1} \approx 0.32$. This establishes a soft attention window, preventing semantic noise from saturating the complex sphere.
